Artificial IntelligenceCSO (AITechnology) and cybersecurity are becoming increasingly intertwined, with AITechnology and Machine LearningCSO (AITechnology/ML) being leveraged for cybersecurity, and cybersecurity helping address issues caused by AITechnology. The goal in our exploratory curricular initiative is to dovetail the need to teach these two critical, emerging topics in highschool, and create a suite of novel activities, 'AITechnology & Cybersecurity for Teens' (ACTTerms) that introduces AITechnology/ML in the context of cybersecurity and prepares high school teachers to integrate them in their cybersecurity curriculaCSO. Additionally, ACTTerms activities are designed such that teachers (and students) build a deeper understanding of how ML works and how the machine actually "learns". Such understanding will aid more meaningful interrogation of critical issues such as AITechnology ethics and bias. ACTTerms introduces core ML topics contextualized in cybersecurity topics through a range of programming activities and pre-programmed games in NetsBlox, an easy-to-use block-based programming environment. We conducted 2 pilot workshops with 12 high school cybersecurity teachers focused on ACTTerms activities. Teachers' feedback was positive and encouraging but also highlighted potential challenges in implementing ACTTerms in the classroom. This paper reports on our approach and activities design, and teachers' experiences and feedback on integrating AITechnology into high school cybersecurity curriculaCSO.
